My Plans for Second Year of Computer Engineering

  • Share:


So its August 2021 and today is my Second Semester End Sem exam :) wierd right! In short my first year of Computer Engineering is about to end.

If you are someone who don’t know what to do in the upcoming year, then you are at the right place. Here are few of my future plans which I will try my best to accomplish.

Base

If you want to be a good programmer, then only thing you should do is….Practice coding daily. This will improve your skills and thought process. The fast you are able to find solution of particular problem, the more are your chances of cracking campus selection interviews.

Don’t hurry to follow the trend

The biggest mistake people do is that they start thinking on which language should they learn first. I must say don’t hesitate to pick C/C++ as your first language in programming.

Many people will say Python is the trend, there is much scope in python and so on. If you learn C/C++ programming first, then moving on towards any other language will not be a big deal for you. For instance, if you learn a manual transmission car, then you will easily get adapt to automatic car.

Keep your understanding and concepts crystal clear, be patient, keep practicing, challenge thyself and you will be a master at programming.

College Curriculum

Depending upon your college syllabus, in each semester you will learn new programming language. Here is the list,

  1. C Language
  2. C++ Programming
  3. Data Structure and Algorithms
  4. Core Java
  5. C#.net
  6. HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  7. PHP
  8. ASP . Net
  9. Android Development (added recently from 2017)
  10. Python

If you want to increase your logical skills then learn Data Structure and Algorithms in very deeply with different languages like C, C++ and Java, it will helps you lot and increase your problem solving skills. Never miss any lecture or any opportunity to learn DSA.

Side by Side

Along with all these, you will have to learn English and aptitude. This will help you to crack campus interviews' first round.

Project Based Learning

Most MNCs are in search of employees who have good practical knowledge.

Projects can be one of the most efficient ways to learn as they force you to apply the skills while learning them. This, in fact, aids in retention and increases the usefulness of the skills learned.

These projects will teach you a great deal about one concept or another. From learning how to apply Algorithms, to learning how to effectively deal with times, you will be able to showcase your skills in most efficient way.

Thank you for reading this far. Comment your views in the comments section. Stay motivated.
